Fauna of Corbett National Park

It’s not every day that the words hunter and conservationist are used in the same sentence to refer to the same person, but that’s exactly who Edward James Corbett was. He was a man who used his hunting prowess for the good of both humankind and the animal kingdom. Born in Nainital during the British Raj, on 25 July 1875, Jim Corbett grew up in close proximity to forests and would embark on excursions that helped him familiarise himself with numerous birds and animals. Because of this close-quarters interaction with wildlife, he became a good hunter and tracker.

The Fauna of Corbett National Park includes a huge list of animals besides Royal Bengal Tigers and Asiatic elephant, which are Asiatic Black Bear, Sloth Bear, Walking Deer, Hog Deer, Sambar, Yellow-throated Marten, Otters and Ghariyal along with aqua fauna such as Golden Oriole, Pallas Fish Eagle, Indian Pitta, Tawny Fish Owl, Scarlet Minivet and species of birds like the Great Pied or hornbill, Orange Breasted Green Pigeon, Hodgson's Bushchat, White-Backed Vulture, and reptile like Ghariyal or Aligator, Mugger or Crocodiles and the King Cobra.
